About the Workshop

At the Missouri Urban Journalism Workshop, high school students from around the U.S. work together with the guidance of professional journalists to produce stories, photos and broadcasts.

Here are some frequently asked questions about the workshop:

What forms and procedures am I required to do before getting to the conference?

Medical and Emergency Contact Information Form: This form provides medical information about the participant as well as contact information for a parent/guardian in case of emergency. You must complete this form in order to attend the workshop. Deadline: May 31, 2019. Indemnification and Medical Treatment Form: This form includes medical and insurance information. You will also be providing any medical information necessary, as well as your emergency contact information. You must complete this form in order to attend the workshop. Deadline: May 31, 2019. Transportation Information Form: Each workshop participants is responsible for making his or her own travel arrangements to and from Columbia, Missouri. Please review carefully all of the information on this form and let us know as soon as possible if there are any changes in your travel plans. Deadline: May 31, 2019. Photo/Video Release Form: This form provides consent for your image and work to be used in publication in any medium and releases the University of Missouri, the Missouri School of Journalism, and the Missouri Urban Journalism Workshop from liability relating to the release of this information. Deadline: May 31, 2019. Workshop Fee: The workshop fee of $650 (your $25 application fee has been applied to your account) is due in full no later than May 31, 2019. If you have been awarded financial aid, you will need to complete the Financial Aid Agreement and return it instead of payment. -Check/money order -Credit/Debit Card

What should I bring?

o Approximately $100 to buy your lunches for the week o Some spending money (for snacks/souvenirs/recreation) o A laptop computer - if you have a laptop it is necessary that you bring it. However, DO NOT purchase one just for the workshop. o At least one flash drive o Writing pens and pencils (we will provide a notebook) o Rainwear and/or umbrella – it usually rains during the workshop, and you will be walking across campus every day o Comfortable shoes – you will do LOTS of walking every day (also, bring Band-Aids for any blisters!) o A blanket for your dorm room – the dorm will provide bed linens, but rooms are air- conditioned and can be cold. o A light jacket or sweater as some rooms on campus are cold o Shower shoes o Cell phone o Students interested in photography should bring their digital camera equipment. Only digital cameras are used during the workshop.

What is the dress code?

Journalism workshop 2019 dress code · Think business casual — not campus casual. Clothes that are too tight, too skimpy or too revealing are not appropriate. · Reporters and photojournalists should not wear shorts or T-shirts while on duty or during extended periods of time in the newsroom. · Jeans can be worn as long as they do not have holes. · Flip flops and casual sandals should not be worn while on duty. · T-shirts, caps and other articles of clothing with emblems, advertisements and Greek insignia are inappropriate for everyone. · Exposed bellies and shoulders are inappropriate. · Jewelry should be tasteful and unobtrusive. · Students will be conducting interviews and, in some cases, doing on-camera work. While dress clothes aren’t necessary, collared shirts and more business-like attire would be suitable.
